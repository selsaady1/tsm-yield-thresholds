4. KEY FINDING — THRESHOLD DIRECTION
--------------------------------------------------
  As the shortage penalty INCREASES:
    -> Thresholds move LEFT (lower FE spread)
    -> Stochastic planning becomes necessary SOONER
    -> Deterministic planning is adequate for a
       NARROWER range of yield uncertainty

  As the shortage penalty DECREASES:
    -> Thresholds move RIGHT (higher FE spread)
    -> Deterministic planning remains adequate for
       a WIDER range of yield uncertainty

7. CONNECTION TO THE LITERATURE
--------------------------------------------------
  Rashidi et al. use a penalty of ~10% of selling
  price in their test case. Our S1 scenario ($1
  penalty on $10 price) matches this ratio. The
  finding that lower penalties push thresholds
  rightward is consistent with the theoretical
  prediction: when shortfalls cost less, the
  asymmetry between over-producing and under-
  producing is smaller, so the deterministic
  model's 'average' plan is less costly.

  This is the second core contribution of the
  ITC 2026 paper: not just where the threshold is
  (Step 5), but how it depends on cost structure
  (Step 6).
