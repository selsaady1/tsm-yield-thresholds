======================================================================
  PRACTITIONER DECISION RULE
  When should you invest in stochastic planning?
======================================================================

  DECISION RULE (primary):
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  If your Front-End yield spread exceeds 0.17,
  invest in stochastic (uncertainty-aware) planning.
  Otherwise, average-yield planning is sufficient.
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  WHAT THIS MEANS IN PRACTICE:

  'Front-End yield spread' is the difference between
  your best-case and worst-case FE yield outcomes.
  For example:
    Spread = 0.17 means yields range from 79.0% to 96.0%
    Spread = 0.10 means yields range from 82.5% to 92.5% (narrow — deterministic is fine)
    Spread = 0.30 means yields range from 72.5% to 100.0% (wide — stochastic is essential)

  REFINED RULE (accounting for cost structure):
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The threshold depends on your shortage penalty:

  Penalty/Price Ratio    Switch when FE spread exceeds
  ───────────────────    ─────────────────────────────
       10%                    0.18   (yields wider than [78.4%, 96.6%])
       30%                    0.18   (yields wider than [78.6%, 96.4%])
       50%                    0.17   (yields wider than [78.8%, 96.2%])
       80%                    0.17   (yields wider than [78.9%, 96.1%])
  ───────────────────    ─────────────────────────────

  INTERPRETATION:
    - Higher shortage penalties → switch sooner
      (threshold moves from 0.18 down to 0.17)
    - The range is narrow (0.17–0.18), meaning the
      threshold is robust: cost structure shifts it
      only slightly.

  IMPORTANT CAVEATS:
    - Back-End yield uncertainty does NOT drive the
      planning approach choice. If your main uncertainty
      is at the Back-End, focus on quality improvement,
      not planning methodology.
    - These thresholds are based on a model with
      FE mean yield of 87.5% and BE mean yield of 80%.
      Different mean yields may shift the thresholds.
    - The model assumes a single product type. Multi-
      product supply chains may have lower thresholds
      due to additional allocation complexity.
